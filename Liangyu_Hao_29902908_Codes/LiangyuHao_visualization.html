Conclusion

The conclusion of this Visualisation Project

T

he project studies the relationship between wildfires and climate in Australia. Through the visualization of temperature, rainfall, humidity and wind guest speed data. In the end, I think that the high temperature and low precipitation make it difficult to extinguish wildfires in the short term. Strong wind guests further contributed to the wildfire, and eventually caused the Australian wildfire to gradually spread across the whole country and burned for up to 5 months.

In January 2020, although the temperature and gust speed have not changed significantly, the rainy season has come to play a vital role in extinguishing wildfires.

The project also tried to analyze the weather factors that led to more wildfires in New South Wales than in other states. However, by comparing the climate factors of various states and researching many website materials, I did not get the reason. I guess that the hot climate, low rainfall, and fast wind speeds cause wildfires. In addition, New South Wales has a large area, high vegetation coverage, and dense population that causes the number of wildfires to be higher than other states.
